Exploring your values

Date: March 21, 2014

When I think of the times in my life that I've been happiest or proudest, the values that come to mind are: Confidence, Enthusiasm, Excellence, Free time, Growth, Quality, Recognition, Personal development, Serenity, Truth and Wisdom.

Growth is important to me for various reasons, one being that without growth, there is no change for the better. For me, I want to try to become the best person I can be. This is not to be confused with being the best at everything I can do. Part of trying to be the best person I can be is growing and learning constantly. Whether that is taking a class to learn something new, or trying new recipes, variety is the spice of life. I like to try new things, and hopefully grow as a person in the process.

In General this is my rating of how I try to live up to these values

  • Confidence: 2
  • Enthusiasm: 3
  • Excellence: 4
  • Free time: 5
  • Growth: 4
  • Quality: 4
  • Recognition: 3
  • Personal development: 3
  • Serenity: 2
  • Truth: 4
  • Wisdom: 2

The last thing that someone asked my advice on was my mother. It was about something minor; she was attending a ballroom dance event and wanted to know which outfit suited her more, and what accessories she should pair with it. My mother frequently asks me advice on fashion and colors.
